What we actually do

Three things. Done with care.

We started because we saw a gap — newcomer students weren't getting the recognition, resources, or community they deserved. So we built something ourselves.

01

Interwoven

Literary Magazine + Oral History

We were tired of immigrant stories being left out of the conversation, so we built our own stage. Interwoven is the first teen multilingual literary magazine dedicated to immigrant voices — poetry, essays, interviews, and more.

Submit your story
02

Opportunities Hub

Resources & Scholarships

Finding scholarships and programs is hard enough without being a newcomer too. We gather the best opportunities in one place — tailored for first-gen students, newcomers, and students of color — so nobody has to search alone.

Explore resources
03

Welcome Kits

Tangible Support

When you're new somewhere, small things mean a lot. We put together kits with translated resources, books, stationery, and little gifts of encouragement — because we want every newly arrived student to feel like someone was expecting them.

Why it's worth it

Honestly? Here's what you get out of this.

We know your time is precious. Here's what you can genuinely expect when you volunteer with us — no fluff.

Real, documented service hours

Writers earn 3–5+ hours per piece. Content creators earn 1–2 hours per post. We verify everything — you can use these hours for PVSA, NHS, college apps, whatever you need.

Work that actually reaches people

Your writing gets published. Your Welcome Kit ends up in a student's hands on their first day. This isn't busy work — the impact is real and you'll feel it.

A path to leadership

Consistent contributors get offered leadership roles. If you want to run a chapter, lead a team, or build something new — we want to hear from you.

A place to share your story

Whether you're an immigrant, a child of immigrants, or just someone who cares — your perspective belongs in Interwoven. We publish in all languages.

Volunteer roles

Find where you fit.

Two main tracks. Pick the one that fits your skills — or do both. Submit 2–3 pieces per month to qualify for service hours.

Role 01

Writer

Share your story or someone else's. Any language, any genre — poetry, essays, interviews, you name it.

"Here I Stand"

Write about your own experience as an immigrant or child of immigrants — through poetry, prose, memoir, or visual art. Your family counts too.

3 hours per piece
"Voices Carried Forward"

Interview someone in your community and document their story. These become part of our national oral history archive.

5+ hours per piece
Role 02

Content Creator

Help us spread the word on social media. We love creative people who know how to make something that stops the scroll.

Resource posts

5–10 slide carousels spotlighting scholarships, programs, and opportunities for newcomers and first-gen students.

1 hour per post
Flyers & graphics

Design promo graphics for Interwoven and our events. Canva or Photoshop experience is a plus, but not required.

2 hours per graphic
Short-form videos

~15 second reels or TikToks adapted to our mission. Keep it natural, honest, and you.

1 hour per video
